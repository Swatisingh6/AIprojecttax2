What is ITR?

ITR (Income Tax Return) is like a report card of your earnings and taxes. It's a form where you tell the government about your income and the taxes you've paid during the year.

üìã Breaking Down ITR

Think of your ITR as a financial story you're telling the government. It includes:

  • Personal Information: Your basic details like name, address, and PAN
  • Income Details: All sources of income (salary, business, investments, etc.)
  • Deductions: Expenses and investments that reduce your taxable income
  • Tax Computation: Calculation of how much tax you owe or how much refund you're entitled to

Did You Know? There are different types of ITR forms (ITR-1, ITR-2, etc.) depending on your income sources and complexity of your financial affairs. Choosing the right form is crucial for accurate filing.

Important Forms Explained

Form 16

A certificate from your employer showing your salary details and taxes deducted. It's like a yearly salary report card.

What's in Form 16?

  • Part A: Contains your basic details, employer details, and TDS summary
  • Part B: Contains detailed salary breakup, deductions, and tax computation

Your employer must issue Form 16 by June 15th of the assessment year. If they delay, you can file a complaint with the Income Tax Department.

Annual Information Statement (AIS)

A comprehensive view of all your financial transactions, including salary, interest, and investments.

What's in AIS?

  • Tax Deducted at Source (TDS)
  • Tax Collected at Source (TCS)
  • Specified Financial Transactions (SFT)
  • Payment of taxes
  • Demand and refund

AIS is more comprehensive than Form 26AS and includes all your financial transactions. Always verify the information in your AIS before filing your return.

Form 26AS

Your tax credit statement. Download it from the Income Tax portal - it shows all taxes deducted on your behalf.

How to Download Form 26AS

  1. Log in to the Income Tax portal (incometax.gov.in)
  2. Go to 'My Account' ‚Üí 'View Form 26AS (Tax Credit)'
  3. Select the assessment year
  4. Download the form in PDF format

Always cross-check the TDS details in Form 26AS with your Form 16. Any mismatch should be reported to your employer or the deductor.

Ready to File? Here's How

Step 1: Register on E-Filing Portal

Visit incometax.gov.in and create your account using your PAN and basic details.

Registration Process

  1. Click on 'Register Yourself' on the homepage
  2. Select user type (Individual)
  3. Enter your PAN, name, and date of birth
  4. Validate your details
  5. Create a password and security questions
  6. Verify your email and mobile number

If you already have an account but forgot your password, use the 'Forgot Password' option. You'll need your PAN, date of birth, and registered email/mobile to reset it.

Step 2: File Your Return

2.1: Choose the Right ITR Form

Select the appropriate ITR form based on your income sources:

  • ITR-1: For individuals with salary, one house property, and other small incomes
  • ITR-2: For individuals with capital gains or foreign assets
  • ITR-3: For individuals and HUFs with business/professional income
  • ITR-4: For presumptive business income

2.2: Fill in Your Details

  1. 1 Validate your personal information
  2. 2 Enter your total income (all earnings combined)
  3. 3 Add your deductions (tax-saving investments)
  4. 4 Verify tax payment details with Form 26AS
  5. 5 Review and confirm your return summary

What is Gross Total Income?

Gross Total Income is the sum of all your income from various sources before applying any deductions. It includes:

  • Salary income
  • House property income
  • Business or professional income
  • Capital gains
  • Other sources (interest, rent, etc.)

Step 3: E-Verify Your Return

Your ITR won't be processed until verified. Choose one method:
